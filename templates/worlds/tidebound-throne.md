# Tidebound Throne

## World Background

The world of Marrowsea is a chain of island nations built around ancient weather engines left by a drowned empire. For centuries, these engines calmed monsoons, guided currents, and made inter-island trade possible. Then one by one, they failed.

Without stable winds, famine and piracy spread. Old monarchies tightened control over ports, while free captains formed floating republics beyond royal law. Beneath the waves, the Abyssal Choir began offering forbidden power: blood-oath rituals that can command storms for a price.

A final engine called the Crown Relay still functions, though erratically. Whoever secures it can tax every shipping lane, starve rivals, and rewrite maritime borders overnight. Court diplomats call this "restoring order." Sailors call it the beginning of the next sea war.

## Geography

### The Marrowsea Archipelago

- **Highwake Crown Isle**: Ceremonial seat of the Tidebound Monarchy and location of the royal naval yards.
- **Rookhaven Shoals**: Dense reef network ruled by pirate syndicates and black-flag councils.
- **Sable Current Passage**: Most profitable trade corridor; also the deadliest ambush route in cyclone season.
- **Choirdeep Trench**: Abyssal fissure where sonar fails and ritual lights are seen below impossible depths.
- **Saltglass Atoll**: Neutral market island where ceasefires are negotiated and betrayed with equal frequency.
- **Stormspire Beacon**: Ruined lighthouse complex that once coordinated all weather engines.

## Maritime Magic and Oath Rules

- **Sea-oath binding**: Spoken vows made over marked water become magically enforceable.
- **Stormcalling rites**: Can redirect weather fronts but often inflict exhaustion or hallucinations.
- **Relic navigation**: Ancient compass-relics reveal safe routes through temporary maelstrom corridors.
- **Tidal debt**: Breaking a sworn maritime oath creates supernatural consequences, from crew mutiny to cursed currents.

## Core Conflicts

- **Crown Relay Succession**: Three competing claimants seek control of the last stable weather engine.
- **Pirate Parliament Split**: Confederacy captains disagree between full war and negotiated autonomy.
- **Chart Cartel Blackmail**: The Guild withholds corrected maps unless granted sovereign privileges.
- **Choir Ascension Rite**: Rumors claim the Abyssal Choir can awaken a storm god beneath Choirdeep.

## DM Guide

### Narrative Tone

- High-seas intrigue with political bargaining, brittle alliances, and volatile weather.
- Travel itself is a challenge; logistics and timing matter.
- Moral choices should affect trade, food supply, and civilian safety.

### NPC Management Rules

- Record key NPCs at first significant encounter.
- Update relationship, influence, and resource fields after treaties, raids, or oath breaks.
- Reflect command changes when fleets fracture or merge.

### Event Management Rules

- Create events for route wars, storm-engine expeditions, blockade crises, and treaty summits.
- Advance events on completed voyages, seized ports, major rituals, and intelligence leaks.
- Resolve events explicitly with ownership, casualties, and trade-route outcomes.

### State Update Rules

Record state updates when:
- weather-engine status changes,
- shipping lanes open/close,
- port control changes,
- food and supply levels fluctuate,
- oath violations trigger supernatural penalties.

### Gameplay Trigger Guide

- **Skill checks**: Use checks for navigation in storms, deck command, oath interpretation, chart forgery detection, and diplomatic mediation.
- **Combat**: Trigger combat for boarding actions, port sieges, mutinies, and ritual interdiction missions.
- **Inventory management**: Track charts, oath tokens, ballast relics, naval permits, cannon powder, and medical salves.
- **Quest tracking**: Manage quests around convoy escorts, sabotage prevention, succession deals, and Crown Relay stabilization.
- **Faction reputation**: Actions should affect trust with monarchists, pirates, guilds, cultists, and local militias.
- **NPC relationships**: Track loyalty shifts after rescue at sea, broken treaties, ransom decisions, and shared spoils.
- **Status effects**: Apply effects such as seasickness, storm shock, oathbound curse, sleep deprivation, and salt rot.
- **Knowledge discovery**: Log discoveries about engine schematics, hidden channels, cult doctrine, and succession evidence.
