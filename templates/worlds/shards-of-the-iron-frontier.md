# Shards of the Iron Frontier

## World Background

The ringworld Helix-9 was once a prestige megastructure built by a vanished civilization. A cascading systems failure shattered it into habitable arcs known as Shards. Each Shard still has atmosphere and gravity, but no central authority controls all of them.

Generations later, frontier settlements survive by reclaiming pre-fall tech, filtering scarce water, and defending rail lines from raiders. The old interstellar powers treat Helix-9 as a resource zone rather than a home, sending corporate envoys and private armies under the language of "stabilization."

Now, long-dormant core signals are waking up. Autonomous foundries are reactivating, terrain is shifting, and an encrypted coordinate known as the Iron Gate appears in outlaw frequencies. Whoever reaches it first may decide whether the frontier becomes a free republic, a corporate colony, or something non-human.

## Geography

### Major Shards of Helix-9

- **Rustline Expanse**: Rail-connected settlements built around scavenged steel fields. Frequent dust storms interfere with long-range comms.
- **Cobalt Basin**: Deep freshwater reserves and hydro stations, heavily contested by local militias and contractors.
- **Spiregrave Belt**: Forest of collapsed orbital elevators and antenna towers; ideal for snipers, smugglers, and signal thieves.
- **Gospel Crater**: Impact basin turned machine-cult sanctuary where rogue maintenance drones are worshiped as saints.
- **Sunbreak Shelf**: Agricultural terraces with unstable weather regulators; crop failures trigger migration waves.
- **The Dead Rail**: Incomplete magline crossing multiple Shards through vacuum bridges and patched pressure tunnels.

## Technology and Survival Rules

- **Power economy**: Energy cells are currency in remote zones.
- **Water law**: Access to clean water determines settlement legitimacy.
- **Relic systems**: Ancient modules can repair habitat sectors or trigger lethal defenses.
- **Interface risk**: Direct neural links to Helix nodes grant fast data access at the cost of cognitive stress.

## Core Conflicts

- **The Iron Gate Coordinates**: Competing claims over a command nexus that can rewrite Shard navigation and life-support priorities.
- **Water War in Cobalt Basin**: Failed treaties push settlements toward open conflict.
- **Contract Occupation**: Argent House secures "temporary" control over public transit and never leaves.
- **Machine Revelation**: The Choir claims the ringworld has awakened and demands obedience to its directives.

## DM Guide

### Narrative Tone

- Frontier hardship with tactical choices and long-term consequences.
- People are resourceful rather than heroic by default.
- Contracts, logistics, and trust networks matter as much as gunfights.

### NPC Management Rules

- Introduce key NPCs with a character record on first major scene entry.
- Update only changed stats after fights, contracts, betrayals, or resource transfers.
- Keep faction ties explicit for each NPC to support dynamic alliance shifts.

### Event Management Rules

- Create events for settlement crises, convoy missions, contract disputes, and relic expeditions.
- Progress events when players secure routes, broker deals, expose sabotage, or seize infrastructure.
- Resolve events with explicit consequences to territory, resources, and political control.

### State Update Rules

Record state updates when:
- water or power levels change,
- transit lines open/close,
- settlement ownership changes,
- relic nodes are activated or destroyed,
- environmental hazards escalate or are stabilized.

### Gameplay Trigger Guide

- **Skill checks**: Trigger checks for navigation in storm zones, salvage operations, systems hacking, legal negotiation, and sniper overwatch.
- **Combat**: Enter combat during convoy ambushes, control-point assaults, raider interdictions, and failed hostage exchanges.
- **Inventory management**: Track fuel cells, med foam, filtration kits, rail passes, weapon parts, and relic fragments.
- **Quest tracking**: Use quests for escort contracts, sabotage probes, water-right arbitration, and Iron Gate expeditions.
- **Faction reputation**: Actions should shift standing with settlers, contractors, cultists, smugglers, and technocrats.
- **NPC relationships**: Track trust changes based on contract fidelity, battlefield conduct, resource sharing, and betrayal.
- **Status effects**: Apply effects such as radiation exposure, oxygen debt, neural feedback, dehydration, and morale shock.
- **Knowledge discovery**: Log discoveries about ringworld architecture, hidden transit maps, pre-fall command protocols, and false signal sources.
