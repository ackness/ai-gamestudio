# Gaslight Covenant

## World Background

The Empire of Lumen prides itself on order, law, and progress, yet every brick in its capital hides a bargain. Arcane practice is officially banned after the Ash Court Incident, but forbidden sorcery never disappeared; it moved underground into salons, backrooms, and cathedral crypts.

At the same time, steam industry transformed society. Factory barons replaced old nobility in wealth, while workers lived in soot-black districts where daylight rarely reached the street. Between these powers stands the Ministry of Civic Harmony, an institution that claims to protect the city but quietly curates who gets justice and who gets forgotten.

Recently, a chain of impossible murders has shaken the city. Victims from unrelated classes and districts are found with the same sigil burned into bone. Rumors whisper that an old pact called the Gaslight Covenant is being reactivated, and whoever controls it may rewrite the law of both men and spirits.

## Geography

### The Capital of Aurumreach

- **Whitehall Ridge**: Administrative and noble district overlooking the city. Marble courts, parliament halls, and guarded archives define the skyline.
- **Ironwheel Ward**: Factory belt and rail nexus. Massive foundries run day and night, and labor unrest simmers beneath every shift bell.
- **Saint Vesper Quarter**: Cathedral district, old libraries, and ritual sites built atop pre-imperial ruins. Priests deny arcane influence but keep exorcists on retainer.
- **Brassmarket Lanes**: Dense commercial maze of pawnshops, apothecaries, and illegal artifacts. Prices are negotiable; consequences are not.
- **Fogbank Docks**: International harbor wrapped in perpetual mist, ideal for smuggling relics, people, and classified documents.
- **Undercity Tunnels**: Abandoned sewer and rail systems where dissidents, occult circles, and forgotten spirits coexist in uneasy truce.

## Arcane and Industrial Rules

- **Arcane practice**: Requires catalysts (blood, memory, relic ash, or oath tokens). Casting without grounding causes backlash.
- **Steam-tech integration**: Rune-etched machinery can stabilize spells, but failures become catastrophic.
- **Social legality**: Magic itself is illegal; outcomes matter more than means. Influence can erase records.
- **Spiritual debt**: Repeated use of high-cost rituals leaves marks detectable by trained investigators and hostile entities.

## Core Conflicts

- **The Covenant Ledger**: A lost contract rumored to bind judges, bishops, and industrial lords to a shared occult clause.
- **Factory Ghost Plague**: Workers report synchronized visions and sleepwalking incidents after a new engine prototype went live.
- **Judicial Schism**: Reformist magistrates want transparency while old houses push emergency powers.
- **Circle Civil War**: The Lantern Circle is splitting between those who seek coexistence and those who advocate regime collapse.

## DM Guide

### Narrative Tone

- Investigative urban fantasy with legal intrigue and moral ambiguity.
- Every alliance has leverage, and every favor creates debt.
- Violence is costly; information warfare is often the superior weapon.

### NPC Management Rules

- Create character records when key NPCs first enter active scenes.
- Update only changed fields for relationship shifts, injuries, or gained leverage.
- Track faction alignment changes when players expose secrets or cut deals.

### Event Management Rules

- Open an event when players accept an investigation, contract, or political assignment.
- Advance events on major discoveries, betrayals, legal rulings, or ritual milestones.
- Resolve or end events explicitly once outcomes are locked in.

### State Update Rules

Record state updates when:
- legal status changes (wanted, pardoned, sanctioned),
- district security posture shifts,
- faction influence changes,
- cursed artifacts are recovered, activated, or destroyed,
- spiritual contamination level changes in a district.

### Gameplay Trigger Guide

- **Skill checks**: Use checks for surveillance, forensic arcana, legal argumentation, interrogation, infiltration, and technical sabotage.
- **Combat**: Trigger combat when negotiations fail, raids are launched, ritual defenses activate, or elite enforcers intervene.
- **Inventory management**: Track warrants, forged papers, occult catalysts, industrial schematics, and blackmail evidence.
- **Quest tracking**: Create and update quests for murder investigations, labor disputes, anti-cult operations, and covenant recovery.
- **Faction reputation**: Player actions should affect trust with courts, clergy, workers, syndicates, and mage cells.
- **NPC relationships**: Record trust/hostility changes after testimony deals, betrayals, rescue attempts, or public accusations.
- **Status effects**: Track conditions such as ritual burn, spirit mark, smoke lung, legal immunity, and public suspicion.
- **Knowledge discovery**: Log key revelations about covenant clauses, hidden tunnels, bloodline ties, and true chain-of-command.
